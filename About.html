About Me

Background

From an early age I have been interesting in programming. My first experience involved attempting to create a 'Rogue' style game within a Windows Command Prompt which I continued to update daily for 3 years.

I studied at the Kings of Wessex Academy on and greatly improved programming knowledge on their Computing (Computer Science) A-Level module.

Experience

After my A-Levels, I decided to against going to university and instead looked locally for jobs in the programming and IT sector.

Fortunately I didn't have to look for long and quickly gained my first role as an IT Support apprentice with Lanier South West, with who I stayed for 5 years.

My role at Lanier progressed from IT Support to Systems Maintenance, Network Infrastructure Management, Internal Software Design and my final role as their Head of IT Systems and Network Management.

After 5 years at Lanier, and rediscovering my passion for Game Development, I decided it was time to move on and applied for a Games Technology course at UWE Bristol.

I achieved a 70+ grade in all modules in my first year and am currently progressing through the second year with the aim to maintain this standard.

Hobbies

My main hobbies are Airsoft and Trading Card Games (CCG), such as Magic the Gathering.

I find Airsoft to be a great hobby due to the fact that it both keeps me fit and active, reinforces manual dexterity and problem solving, and is also a great way to socialize.

Fortunately, during my first year at UWE I became involved with the UWE Airsoft Society, participating in weekly evening and weekend events as a group.
