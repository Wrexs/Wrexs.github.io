Gameplay Programmer Role Research

Updated 24th Feb 2021 -  Zachery Pavey

Role Description:

The main focus of the gameplay programmer is to create / code the interactions for game systems to a standard provided by a designer(s).


Screenskills lists a core responsibility as:
"They work with level designers to see what needs to be done to make the gameplay work. They write the rules that govern what objects do and pay attention to balancing and tuning of the way the game plays. They also fix bugs and optimise the game for playing.”

Salaries for a gameplay programmer vary from £25k to £49k in the UK, as listed by glassdoor, with the average being around £31k.

A gameplay programmer has room for broadening their career to other roles. As a gameplay programmer you can progress to a senior position, with more responsibility within the project along with direction junior programmers on project scope / tasks.

Alternatively, a gameplay programmer can easilly shift into other programming or even design roles. Such as Lead Designer for systems if working on an ongoing or continual project such as an MMO.

In summary, the gameplay programmer is responsible managing and creating the logic upon which the game is based and plays a central role to completing a finished product and has scope to progress to additional roles within the games industry. 

If you are looking to create the logic behind a game's core systems then the role of Gameplay Programmer could be right for you. 

Where To Look:

There are many places where one can look for available jobs within the games industry. Commonly used websites such as Indeed.co.uk and Jobsite.co.uk feature many listings for gameplay programmer jobs. Additionally these sites often list related jobs such as level design, AI development and smaller projects and experiences that require the use of a game engine, such as interactive experiences.

Most listings will include required and/or desired skills for the applicant to possess. The most common of these are:

  • Experience within the industry.
  • Shipped multiple titles.
  • Proficiency with relevant langues and/or engines.
  • Teamworking skills.

Another tool avaible to those looking for a job within the games industry is gamesindustry.biz, which is by both small and large production developers to advertise positions for current projects.

Finally, there is another option which is to contact developers directly. Many studios / developers will list currently available positions on their websites. This can usually be found on a 'Careers' or 'Jobs' page.

Who Is Hiring:

Many developement studios are currently listing positions for gameplay programmers such as:

  • Blizzard Entertainment - Experience Required
  • Riot Games - Graduate Entry (Relocation offered)
  • Splash Damage - Graduate Entry
  • Junkfish - Graduate Entry
  • Creative Assembly - Many Roles offered depending on experience

Some studios have team to relocate new employees either closer to their offices or even to another country. e.g from the UK to USA.
Due to this it can be beneficial to look for positions listed in other countries and investigate whether the company offers relocation with the role.

Advice:

To summarise I will offer a few tips when searching for and applying for jobs within the games industry:

  • Have a publically accessible portfolio. -> It is essential for an applicant to be able to demonstrate their skills and experience to an employer. By creating a publically accessible portfolio of previous projects you can provide evidence of your experience and skillset when applying for positions.
  • Apply for positions even if you do not meet the requirements. - > Some employers are willing to accept applicants that show confidence and dedication, along with evidence of skill, over those that fit all listed requirements. e.g. 1 year of experience vs 3 years of experience.
  • Contact developers if your position is not listed. -> In some circumstances a developer may not be advertising for an available position. For example an employer could be listing a senior programmer role but have positions open for junior programmers.
