About Me

Background

From an early age I have been interesting in programming. My first experience involved attempting to create a 'Rogue' style game within a Windows Command Prompt which I continued to update daily for 3 years.

I studied at the Kings of Wessex Academy and greatly improved my programming knowledge on their Computing (Computer Science) A-Level module.

Experience

After my A-Levels, I decided against going to university and instead looked locally for jobs in the programming and IT sector.

Fortunately, I didn't have to look for long and gained my first role as an IT Support apprentice with Lanier South West, with who I stayed for 6 years.

My role at Lanier progressed from IT Support to Systems Maintenance, Network Infrastructure Management, Internal Software Design and my final role as their Head of IT Systems and Network Management.

After 6 years at Lanier, and rediscovering my passion for Game Development, I decided it was time to change fields and applied for the Games Technology course at UWE Bristol.

Whilst at UWE I explored many aspects of games development with a personal focus on Virtual Reality.

Hobbies

My main hobbies include Airsoft and Trading Card Games, such as Magic the Gathering.

I find Airsoft to be a great hobby due to the fact that it both keeps me active, reinforces manual dexterity and problem solving in a fast-paced environment.

During my years at UWE I was involved with the UWE Airsoft Society, participating in evening and weekend events as a group.

Participating in events with the UWE Airsoft team.
